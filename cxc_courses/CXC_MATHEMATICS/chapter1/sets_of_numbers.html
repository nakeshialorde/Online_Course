Sets of Numbers

Natural Numbers
The set of counting numbers, that is, ℕ={1,2,3,4...}.
Whole Numbers
The set of positive integers or zero.
Integers
The set consisting of the positive and negative whole numbers and zero, ℤ={...,-2,-1,0,1,2...}. All integers are rational numbers.
Rational Numbers
Numbers that can be expressed as the quotient of two integers (that it any number that can be expressed as a fraction). For examples, 7⁄3, 5⁄11, -5⁄13 or p⁄q, where p and q are integers, q≠0.
Irrational Numbers
A number that cannot be represented as an exact ratio of two integers. For examples, π or √2.
Real Numbers
The union of the set of rational numbers and the set of irrational numbers. Real numbers are represented by the symbol ℝ.
