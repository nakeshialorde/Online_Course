Chapter 7 Index


Relations, Functions and Graphs

General Objectives

On completion of this Section, students should:
Appreciate the importance of relations in Mathematics;
Appreciate that many mathematical relations may be represented in symbolic, tabular or pictorial form; and,
Appreciate the usefulness of concepts in relations, functions and graphs to solve real-world problems.

1 - Concepts of Relations

Concept of a relation, types of relations, examples and non-examples of relations, domain, range, image, co-domain.

2 - Representing Relations

Set of ordered pairs, arrow diagrams, graphically, algebraically.

3 - Functions

Concept of a function, examples and non-examples of functions.

4 - Functional Notation

For example, ƒ∶ x → x2; or ƒ(x) = x2as well as y = ƒ(x) for given domains. The inverse function ƒ-1(x). Composite functions ƒg = ƒ[g(x)].

5 - Distinguish Between Functions and Relations

Ordered pairs, arrow diagram, graphically (vertical line test).

6 - Graphs of Linear Functions

Concept of linear function, types of linear function (y = c; x = k; y = mx + c; where m, c and k are real numbers).

7 - Intercepts of Graphs of Linear Functions

x-intercepts and y-intercepts, graphically and algebraically.

8 - Gradient of a Straight Line

Definition of gradient/slope.

9 - Equation of a Straight Line

Using:(a) the graph of the line; (b) the co-ordinates of two points on the line; (c) the gradient and one point on the line; (d) one point on the line or its gradient, and its relationship to another line.

10 - Gradient of Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

Solve problems involving the gradient of parallel and perpendicular lines;

11 - Co-ordinates on a line Segment

The concept of magnitude or length, concept of midpoint.

12 - Simultaneous Linear Equations

Intersection of graphs.

13 - Linear Inequalities

Represent the solution of linear inequalities in one variable using: (a) set notation; (b) the number line; and, (c) graph.

14 - Graphs of Linear Inequalities

Draw a graph to represent a linear inequality in two variables;

15 - Linear Programming

Use linear programming techniques to graphically solve problems involving two variables;

16 - Composition of Functions

Composite function of no more than two functions, for example, fg, f2 given f and g. Noncommutativity of composite functions (fg, ≠ gf,) in general.

17 - Relationship Between a Function and Its Inverse

The concept of the inverse of a function; The composition of inverse functions f(x) and f−1(x) is commutative and results in x.

18 - Inverse of a Function

Evaluate a function f(x) at a given value of x. f(a), f-1(a), fg(a), where a ∈ ℝ.

19 - Evaluating a Function

f-1, (fg)-1.

20- Roots of an Equation

Draw and use the graph of a quadratic function to identify its features.

21 - Graph of a quadratic function

Concepts of gradient of a curve at a point, tangent, turning point. Roots of the function.

22 - Equation of the axis of symmetry

Determine the equation of the axis of symmetry and the maximum or minimum value of a quadratic function expressed in the form a(x + h)2 + k.

23 - Sketching the graph of a quadratic

Sketch the graph of a quadratic function expressed in the form y = a(x + h)2 + k and determine the number of roots

24- Graphs of non-linear functions

y = axn where n = -1, -1 and +3 and a is a constant. Including distance-time and speed-time.

25 - Interpreting Graphs of Functions

Distance-time graphs and speed-time graphs.

26 - Linear and Non-Linear Functions

Solve problems involving graphs of linear and non-linear functions.
