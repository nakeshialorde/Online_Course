Chapter 6 Index


Algebra

General Objectives

On completion of this Section, students should:
Appreciate the use of algebra as a language and a form of communication;
Appreciate the role of symbols and algebraic techniques in solving problems in mathematics and related fields; and,
Demonstrate the ability to reason with abstract entities.


1 - Symbolic Representation

Use symbols to represent numbers, operations, variables and relations.

2 - Algebraic Symbols and Worded Expressions

Translate between algebraic symbols and worded expressions.

3 - Arithmetic Operations

Evaluate arithmetic operations involving directed numbers.

4 - Algebraic Expressions

Simplify algebraic expressions using the four basic operations.

5 - Substitution

Substitute numbers for variables in algebraic expressions.

6 - Binary Operations

Evaluate expressions involving binary operations (other than the four basic operations); Commutative, associative and distributive properties.

7 - Distributive Law

Apply the distributive law to factorise or expand algebraic expressions; For example, x(a + b) = ax + bx and (a + b)(x + y)
= ax + bx + ay + by..

8 - Algebraic Fractions

The four basic operations on algebraic fractions.

9 - Law of Indices

Use the laws of indices to manipulate expressions with integral indices;
For m ∈ ℤ, n ∈ ℤ. (i) xm x xn = xm+n, (ii)xm⁄ xn = xm-n, (iii) (xm)n = xm+n and (iv) x-m = 1⁄xm.

10 - Linear Equations

Solve linear equations in one unknown.

11 - Simultaneous Linear Equations

Solve simultaneous linear equations, in two unknowns, algebraically.

12 - Simple Linear Inequality

Solve a simple linear inequality in one unknown.

13 - Formulae

Including equations involving roots and powers.

14 - Algebraic Expressions

Factorise algebraic expressions of the type: a2 - b2; a2 ± 2ab + b; ax + bx + ay + by; ax2 + bx + c where a, b, and c are integers and a ≠ 0.

15 - Quadratic Expressions

Rewrite a quadratic expression in the form a(x+h)2 + k.Completing the square of a quadratic expression.

16 - Quadratic Equations

Formula and methods of factorisation and completing the square.

17 - Word Problems

Linear equation, Linear inequalities, two simultaneous linear equations, quadratic equations. Applications to other subjects for example demand and supply functions of business studies.

18 - Pair of Equations

Solve a pair of equations in two variables when one equation is quadratic or nonlinear and the other linear.

19 - Equations vs. Identities

Prove two algebraic expressions to be identical.

20- Direct and Inverse Variation

Represent direct and inverse variation symbolically. y varies directly as x: y ∝ x, y = kx, y varies inversely as x: y ∝ 1⁄x, y=k⁄x.

21 - Solving Problems Involving Direct and Inverse Variation

Solve problems involving direct variation and inverse variation.
