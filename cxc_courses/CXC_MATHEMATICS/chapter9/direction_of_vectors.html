Concept of a Vector

Click on the card behind

  • Vectors

    A quantity a (represented by arrows) that have both a length (magnitude) and direction. The length of a vector is the distance from its initial point P to its final point Q (These points can also be represented by A for head and B for tail). A vector is represented by AB → , the arrow above the components indicates that it is a vector quantity.

    Example of a Vector

    The tail of a vector is its origin and the head is the endpoint.Vectors can be in component form (a,b) or in the coordinate plane.

  • Magnitude

    The magnitude of a vector AB → refers to its length and is represented by
    ∣ AB → ∣.The length of a vector is determined by Pythagoras' Theorem whereby AB → = √x2 + y2. After finding the square root of the two components which can also be represented by √a2 + b2, the result is simply added to get the magnitude or length of the vector in component form or in the plane.



    The magnitude is the length from the tail to the head of the vector v⃗.




  • Unit Vector

    Vector of length 1 or ∣u∣ = 1. A unit vector can start with any vector except the zero vector. The formula for the unit vector AB → is obtained by dividing a vector by a magnitude, u = AB⃗ ∕ ∣ AB → ∣.Checking to ensure that the length of u is really 1, ∣u∣ = ∣ AB → ∕ ∣AB∣ = ∣ AB → ∕ AB → ∣ = 1.



  • Direction

    The direction of a vector AB → is obtained by finding the angle of the vector. The angle is obtained using the following formula, θ = tan -1 ₍b ∕ a₎.





  • Scalar

    A scalar is a real number, while vectors are not real numbers. A scalar has a size or length but no direction, while vectors have both a length and direction.

    Scalar Multiples

    Multiplying a scalar by a positive number does not affect its direction of the vector, just its length. However, multiplying by a negative number changes the direction to the opposite side, multiplying by 0 causes a vector to lose all direction.


  • Parallel Vectors

    A parallel vector or zero vector comprises of two vectors with the same direction and are scalar multiples of each other.The cross product of a vector determines whether it is parallel, if it equals 0 or a×b = 0. The scalar multiply or cross product of two or more vectors is represented by the formula = (a2b3 − a3b2 ) − (a1b3 − a3b1) + (a1b2 − a2b1).
    ∴ a = (1,2,3), b = (3,2,1) then a×b = (2 x 1 - 3 x 2) - (1 x 1 - 3 x 3) + (1 x 2 - 2 x 3) = (-4) - (-8) + (-4) = 0.
